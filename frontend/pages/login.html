Description of Services

The purpose of this System is to demonstrate core principles of Information Assurance and Security, including data protection, authentication, access control, and secure system integration. It serves the EVSU Ormoc Campus by providing CRCY student responders with instant, secure access to student-approved medical directives via tokenized QR codes during campus medical emergencies.

Limitation of Use

The System is subject to the following limitations:

  • Hardware Dependency: Responders must have a functioning, charged smartphone to run the scanner.
  • Connectivity Required: The application requires active EVSU intranet/internet connectivity to query the API and load the student profile.
  • Physical Tag Dependency: Students must have their physical QR tag on their person during an emergency.
  • Admin-Mediated Data: Students cannot update profiles directly. All data updates are entered by clinic admins based on physical consent forms.

Prohibited Use of the Services

  • Attempt unauthorized access to administrative API endpoints or mobile scanner views outside your Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) permissions.
  • Create spoofed or manually altered QR codes to deceive the cryptographic verification systems (HMAC-SHA256).
  • Execute brute-force login attacks against the Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) mechanisms.
  • Extract or copy raw medical text maliciously without a signed responder session.

Use of Services

By using the System, you agree to comply with all security implementations. Active responder sessions are strictly monitored and expire after 30 days to facilitate continuous availability. Clinic admins hold a remote kill switch to immediately deactivate any responder and invalidate their active session tokens in the event of a lost device or unauthorized access.

Privacy Policy

The System adheres strictly to the right-to-erasure and notification principles of RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012). Students must opt-in by submitting a physical medical consent form. Upon registration, students receive an automated email summarizing their encoded profile and providing a one-click tag deactivation link. All data in transit is protected over secure TLS/HTTPS configurations, and sensitive credentials at rest are strongly hashed utilizing bcrypt and SHA-256.
